Profile


I am an Economist at the OECD Centre for Entrepreneurship, SMEs, Regions and Cities (CFE). My main research interests are applied econometrics, environmental economics, industrial development, Input-Output analysis and structural change.

I have a PhD from the University of Nottingham and previously studied at Duke University, the University of Manchester as well as the Vienna University of Economics and Business (WU). I hold graduate degrees in economics and econometrics.

Before joining the OECD, I worked as a Research Associate at the UCL Energy Institute and a Teaching Associate at the University of Nottingham. During my PhD, I completed a one-year traineeship at the European Central Bank (ECB), Directorate General Research (DG/R) and the Price-setting Microdata Analysis Network (PRISMA).

I have collaborated with the World Bank and the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) on multiple research and policy projects and held the positions of Research Analyst at the ECB and Research Assistant at the Social Science Research Institute (SSRI) at Duke University.
